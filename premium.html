How the number is made: per-at-bat hit chance starts from a 60/40 blend of Statcast expected batting average (xBA) and actual average, regressed to league average by sample size. It's then adjusted for the batter's split vs the pitcher's hand, last-15-games form, the pitcher's batting-average-against (vs the batter's side), the pitch-type matchup (batter's xBA against each pitch in the pitcher's arsenal, weighted by how often he throws it, capped at ±7%), and ballpark. That per-AB chance is compounded over the batter's expected at-bats for their lineup slot: P(hit) = 1 − (1 − p)^AB.

Honesty note: even elite hitters record a hit in only ~75–80% of their games. A "lock" here means ~78%+, not a guarantee — anyone promising 90%+ hit locks is selling you noise. The tick mark on each bar is the league baseline (~66%): the fill past the tick is the batter's real edge.
